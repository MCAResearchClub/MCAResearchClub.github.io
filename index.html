Student Projects

All NJ high school students are invited to bring their posters and research for others to see. Professors judge these posters based on their respective fields followed by an open audience viewing. The quality of these projects improve every year with one student presenting a project that qualified for ISEF.

Networking and Learning

Free periods are sprinkled throughout the event allowing students to network with professors, asking for research opportunities, guidance on their projects, or general advice. 8th graders are invited to observe the event but are not able to present their own research. All students can talk directly to these professors and engage with them.

We encourage and guide all students to participate in local NJ research fairs/conventions including the Terra North Jersey Science Fair (TNJSF), Junior Science and Humanities Symposium (JSHS), and MIT Undergraduate Research & Technology Conference (URTC).

TNJSF Team Success

The establishment of the Research Convention has brought an all time high in TNJSF involvement. We carefully guide participants through the fair process, introducing underclassment to the research process and connecting students with mentors to review their work. For the year of 2024, out of 10 students, 1 of our students were sent as an alternate for ISEF!

URTC Success

The Research club encouraged students to apply to URTC this year, achieving the greatest amount of student presentations in the Edison Academy's history. Over 20 students out of a 42 student class presented either a lightning talk or poster at MIT. 2 students were published in MIT's IEEE Xplore journal and one of our teams recieved the "best lightning talk" award.

Computer Science

Research in computer science encompasses a vast and dynamic landscape of exploration and innovation. It delves into the realms of artificial intelligence, machine learning, cryptography, data science, and more, aiming to solve complex problems, advance technology, and reshape the way we interact with computers and data. Researchers in this field continually push boundaries, developing algorithms that can understand human language, optimizing systems for efficiency and security, and creating cutting-edge applications that impact various industries. Computer science research plays a pivotal role in shaping the future of technology, making it an ever-evolving, exciting field that drives progress and transforms possibilities in the digital age.

Biology and Chemistry

Research in biology and chemistry is a dynamic and essential field that explores the intricacies of life and the fundamental processes that govern the natural world. In biology, researchers delve into the realms of genetics, ecology, physiology, and beyond, uncovering the mysteries of DNA, the complexities of ecosystems, and the mechanisms that underlie living organisms. Concurrently, in chemistry, scientists investigate the properties, interactions, and transformations of matter, pushing the boundaries of our understanding of molecules and their reactions. Together, these disciplines contribute to advancements in medicine, environmental conservation, and the development of novel materials, offering profound insights into the fabric of life and the physical world.

Engineering

Research in engineering is a dynamic and multifaceted field that focuses on advancing technological solutions to address complex real-world challenges. Engineers engage in systematic investigations, experimentation, and innovation to push the boundaries of knowledge and improve existing systems or develop entirely new ones. From aerospace engineering, where scientists explore cutting-edge materials and designs for more efficient aircraft, to biomedical engineering, where researchers seek innovative healthcare solutions, the breadth of engineering research is vast. This research not only fosters technological progress but also enhances our understanding of the natural world, ultimately shaping the way we live and interact with our environment.

Physics

Research in physics is a relentless pursuit of understanding the fundamental laws that govern the universe and pushing the boundaries of human knowledge. Physicists delve into the mysteries of particle physics, cosmology, quantum mechanics, and relativity, among other fields, through meticulous experimentation, mathematical modeling, and theoretical exploration. This endeavor aims to unravel the deepest secrets of matter, energy, space, and time, with potential implications spanning from the smallest subatomic particles to the vast expanse of the cosmos. These endeavors not only broaden our comprehension of the natural world but also drive technological innovations that impact our daily lives, making physics research an invaluable and dynamic field at the forefront of human exploration and discovery.

Mathematics

Research in mathematics is a dynamic and ever-evolving field that delves into the profound intricacies of numbers, shapes, and patterns. Mathematicians explore a vast spectrum of topics, from pure and abstract concepts like number theory and algebraic geometry to applied areas such as cryptography, optimization, and data analysis. Through rigorous investigation, mathematical research seeks to uncover the fundamental principles that underlie the natural world and technological innovations. It involves developing new theories, proving conjectures, and creating novel algorithms that have far-reaching implications in various industries, including physics, economics, and computer science. Mathematics research is the driving force behind countless scientific breakthroughs and plays a pivotal role in shaping our understanding of the universe and our ability to solve complex real-world problems.

Economics

Research in economics encompasses a vast and dynamic field that delves into the intricate workings of markets, consumer behavior, and the allocation of resources. Economists employ various methodologies, from rigorous mathematical models to empirical analyses, to examine critical issues such as economic growth, income inequality, inflation, and fiscal policy. This research aims to not only unravel the complexities of economic systems but also provide actionable insights for policymakers, businesses, and individuals. Whether investigating the impact of trade policies on international markets or exploring the behavioral economics of decision-making, economists play a pivotal role in shaping our understanding of the global economy and devising strategies to address its challenges.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you have a question that we haven't gotten the chance to answer yet? Email: mcaresearchclub@gmail.com

How do I join the Research Club?

Visit our booth at club fair and join our club classroom using code: auou6lc on your school account. We post weekly updates, so make sure you're in the classroom!

How do I get started with Research?

We recommend that you gain a solid understanding in the field you are attempting to do research in before you dive headfirst into research. Trying to join a research laboratory or project without the necessary experiences can be premature and invaluable to you. Use the resources we provide here, on our google classroom, and on the Internet to develop a strong conceptual understanding of your research topic of interest.

How do I cold-email?

We've released a full length guide on getting started with research and cold-emailing templates on google classroom! Head to the resources section to find all of our tips-and-tricks designed specifically for you.
